Basic Lessons - Part 3 - Tips to learn french by podcast or BBC learn french

How many times have you arrived in Paris for a leisurely visit, suddenly paralyzed by a well known tourist trauma known simply as: Fear of French. The FOF antidote is relatively painless and as accessible as an mp3 player or iPod (make that eePod if you want to pronounce it correctly in French.)

In the last two years, a small online industry has bloomed that offers free, practical podcasts in the French language targeting beginners to advanced students. My favorites are short lessons to cope with practical situations from a dental visit to making reservations at a swank restaurant. But I also like the podcasts that offer quick postcard stories about life in France. The lessons make long flights or train rides into swift commutes.

A sampling:

Learn French by Podcast
These free podcasts are clear, professional and extremely practical with explanations in English and French about coping with the daily routines of life. The lessons tend to be aimed at intermediate to advanced French students.

RFI français facile, daily news and headlines from France that are delivered in slow mode by the international French broadcaster. RFI also offers exercises and vocabulary to improve comprehension.

Français Facile is a Web site from “Vincent” offering daily podcasts aimed at students from beginners to advanced. You’ll find the basics here, but also can explore eclectic topics from making a crepe to stirring up squash soup.

One Thing in a French Day I adore this Web site because of its snapshots of French life delivered by “Laeticia” who gives two to three minute snippets of her daily life from losing her favorite neighborhood butcher to watching the patterns of swallows circling by her baby’s window.
